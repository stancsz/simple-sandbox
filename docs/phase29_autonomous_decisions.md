# Phase 29: Advanced Autonomous Decision Making

## Overview
Phase 29 extends the agency's autonomy by introducing the capability to handle complex business decisions that traditionally require human judgment. By leveraging the Economic Engine (Phase 24), Corporate Consciousness (Phase 25), and optimized infrastructure (Phase 28), the system can now gather data, run financial and strategic simulations, and execute decisions within predefined policy boundaries.

## Architecture

The Autonomous Decision Making system operates on a set of core components:

1. **Partnership Evaluation Engine**: Tools that assess potential partnerships against the current Corporate Strategy, compute market impact, and project financial viability using established metrics.
2. **Policy Engine Extensions**: The `CorporatePolicy` schema has been extended with the `autonomous_decision_authority` block. This block defines strict boundaries (e.g., maximum contract values, risk scores, alignment thresholds) within which the LLM is allowed to make autonomous decisions without human intervention.
3. **Immutable Audit Trail**: All decisions (whether approved, rejected, or escalated) are recorded as `autonomous_decision` events in the Episodic Memory (Brain), complete with the full context (financials, strategy alignment, and policy notes).

## Tools

### `evaluate_partnership_opportunity`
Evaluates a potential partnership opportunity by pulling the latest Corporate Strategy and Market Data. The LLM acts as the Chief Business Officer, generating a recommendation (`approve`, `reject`, or `escalate`).

Crucially, after the LLM generates a recommendation, the tool enforces the active policy bounds. If the LLM attempts to approve a partnership that exceeds the `max_contract_value`, violates the `allowed_risk_score`, or falls below the `auto_approve_threshold`, the decision is autonomously forced to `escalate` with a documented policy violation note.

### `simulate_partnership_outcomes`
Simulates the financial outcomes of a proposed partnership. It calculates raw financial metrics (Total Projected Revenue, Net Profit, and ROI) based on expected inputs and then uses the LLM (acting as a Financial Analyst) to assess the financial viability and recommend next steps (`proceed`, `renegotiate`, or `abandon`).

### `update_operating_policy` (Extended)
The `update_operating_policy` tool now accepts configuration for `max_contract_value`, `allowed_risk_score`, and `auto_approve_threshold`. These parameters dictate the agency's operational risk limits for autonomous contract approvals.

## Example Flow

1. **Policy Update**: The C-Suite defines that the agency can autonomously approve partnerships up to $100,000 with a "medium" risk tolerance and an alignment score > 80.
2. **Proposal Analysis**: A lead is discovered for a $50,000 partnership with a "low" risk profile.
3. **Evaluation**: `evaluate_partnership_opportunity` assesses the lead against the strategy. It scores an 95 on alignment.
4. **Enforcement**: The policy engine confirms the metrics fall within the autonomous boundaries.
5. **Execution**: The partnership is given an `approve` recommendation.
6. **Logging**: The entire decision context is serialized into Brain memory for future retrospective analysis.

## Policy Engine Safeguards

The system operates with a "secure by default" posture. If no `autonomous_decision_authority` policy explicitly allows auto-approval, any "approve" recommendation generated by the LLM is automatically downgraded to "escalate" for human review.
